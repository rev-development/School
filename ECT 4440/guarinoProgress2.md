Between Progress Report #1 and #2, I've primarily been working on the actual story part of the game and I'm very proud of my progress on it so far.

I had the goal of finishing it in two to three weeks, I didn't quite hit that because of the rough start to my group but I am matching the pace I intended and will have it done well before the next progress check.

Getting the plot points mapped out has made things much, much easier. Its eliminated the need for open-ended or aimless writing which I think is a huge reason writing projects don't get done. Now, I just jump in and write a handful of passages and slowly fill in my plot map.

Additionally, for my personal pride, I want to demonstrate that I'm actually capable of writing a quality narrative off the bat. I kind of got the impression early on that doing a visual novel / Telltale style game seemed like it spelled doom. My Dad is an author and I'd love to have published work, even if it is via game.

This project is mechanically more simple than my other work because I wanted to emphasize the polishing steps of Game Design, I've done plenty of technically challenging games. I want to work on things I haven't touched before like good audio queueing, reading and writing save files, and actual user interface. They aren't the most exciting part of game development, but they are things between me and then finish line that I have yet to interact with.

The simplicity also means that any element included in the game needs to be stellar. I could make the group's progress look better by making some flashy UI in Unity but I do not want to end up with a half-baked choose-your-own-adventure game because the story was an afterthought. I know from the teacher perspective you'll probably hose my grade on this and start raising red flags because the majority of the work is outside of Unity and the actual game. However, if the story sucks, this game is a travesty.

But like, I've seen one piece of art she's made. And I know that I'm not underestimating the time it takes to make art because we specifically developed an aesthetic style to solve the issue of needing lots of art. It's black and white, minimalist, action item focused art. There aren't fine details or figures, its a game about the lines you see when you close your eyes, things are scribbly. In fact, the monsters in the world are described as scribbly and blurry.

I want to be miffed about it, but I also know that there are things I don't know. Like, maybe I've pointed her in an artistic direction that doesn't support rapidity. I know that not everyone componentizes ideas and concepts like I do and part of me worries that when I showed her Frank Miller as inspiration, she took away the level of detail and precision rather than the simple color palette and the use of negative space so you don't have to draw as much. But I really think she's just not setting aside time to do it.
